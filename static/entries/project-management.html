Introduction

This workshop was lead by Bilyana Balkanska and was about the world of project management and all the different techniques and methodologies that are used. In the end, we were able to put into practice what we had learned with a project.

Overview

The workshop consisted of a very detailed and engaging presentation from Bilyana at the start, along with splitting the cohort into two teams. We all learnt about the different project management techniques such as the waterfall lifecycle, as well as the idea of scope management, and ensuing you don't go out of cost, time or quality. In addition, we also learnt about PESTLE and how to use it effectively. After the presentation, each group (ours being called Spades) was tasked with designing a pop-up café and to present our idea to the other group.


Challenges

The main challenge at first was being able to compromise within such a large group and decide on what ideas to incorporate, though after a while we got the hang of it and were able to include everyone's thoughts while still keeping everyone on the same page. In addition, while it wasn't a major problem, we did struggle a bit with the time management, ensuring that we had time to practice our presentation at the end.


Solution

Our solution was to create a sustainable matcha café called Sippin' Green which would be targeted towards young people and their love for matcha. During this, we learnt valuable skills to do with managing a large group of people as well as presenting to an even larger group of people.


Result
